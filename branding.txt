Mission Statement
	To give every young person south of the river access to hands-on technology education — building the confidence, creativity and computational thinking they need to shape their own future.

Vision Statement
	A future where no child in our community misses out on the opportunity to become a creator of technology, not just a consumer of it.

Brand Values
These underpin everything — the programme design, the pricing, the way you communicate with parents:
Accessible — Quality STEM education shouldn't require a drive to the northern suburbs. We bring it to the communities that need it most.
Hands-on — Students build, break, debug and rebuild. Learning happens through doing, not watching.
Progressive — Every camp is a stepping stone. There is always a next level — in our programme and in life.
Teacher-led — Real curriculum expertise, not a script. Every session is designed and delivered by someone who understands how young people learn.
Ownership — Students leave with something real — a device, a skill, a sense of what they're capable of.

Tagline Options
A few directions for the one-liner that sits under the logo:
	• "Start at base camp. Code to the summit." — tells the full story
	• "Where young coders begin their climb." — warm, aspirational
	• "Learn it. Build it. Keep it." — punchy, references the micro:bit takeaway
	• "Your first line of code starts here." — speaks directly to beginners
My pick is the first one — it uses the brand name's own metaphor and sets up the pathway idea perfectly.

What Comes Next for the Business Plan
Now that we have the brand identity locked, the business plan will need these sections:
	1. Executive summary — one page pitch using the mission above
	2. Market analysis — the gap south of the river, competitor landscape (Code Camp, etc.)
	3. Programme offering — the 3-day camp structure, pricing, hardware costs
	4. Operations plan — venues, staffing, cohort sizes, holiday schedule
	5. Marketing strategy — how you reach parents (Facebook groups, school newsletters, libraries)
	6. Financial projections — revenue per cohort, break-even, Year 1 vs Year 2 targets
